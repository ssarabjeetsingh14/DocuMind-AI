STATISTICS NOTES FOR DATA SCIENCE

1.  Introduction to Statistics Statistics is the science of collecting,
    analyzing, interpreting, and presenting data. Two main types:

-   Descriptive Statistics: Summarizes data (mean, median, mode, etc.)
-   Inferential Statistics: Makes predictions or inferences about a
    population from a sample.

2.  Types of Data

-   Qualitative (Categorical): Non-numeric (e.g., gender, color)
-   Quantitative (Numerical): Numeric
    -   Discrete: Countable (e.g., number of students)
    -   Continuous: Measurable (e.g., height, weight)

3.  Measures of Central Tendency

-   Mean: Average value
-   Median: Middle value
-   Mode: Most frequent value

4.  Measures of Dispersion

-   Range: Max - Min
-   Variance: Spread of data
-   Standard Deviation: Square root of variance

5.  Probability Basics Probability = Favorable outcomes / Total outcomes

-   Independent events
-   Conditional probability

6.  Distributions

-   Normal Distribution (Gaussian)
-   Binomial Distribution
-   Poisson Distribution

7.  Sampling

-   Random Sampling
-   Stratified Sampling
-   Systematic Sampling

8.  Hypothesis Testing

-   Null Hypothesis (H0)
-   Alternative Hypothesis (H1)
-   p-value
-   Significance level (alpha)

9.  Correlation vs Causation

-   Correlation: Relationship between variables
-   Causation: One variable causes another

10. Regression

-   Linear Regression
-   Logistic Regression

11. Bayes Theorem P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

12. Central Limit Theorem The sampling distribution of the mean
    approaches normal distribution as sample size increases.

13. Key Terms

-   Population vs Sample
-   Bias and Variance
-   Overfitting and Underfitting

End of Notes
